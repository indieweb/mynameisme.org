My name is Heidi Waterhouse

My name is Heidi Waterhouse. I am a technical writer, a Sunday school teacher, a knitter, and a believer in online privacy. My spouse and I met online and fell in love. In time we got married and had two children, who we are raising as digital natives.

Before they were born, my children had Amazon wishlists and email accounts. Before they could read, they played online games. And before we set them loose on the internet, we explained over and over again that they should never ever tell anyone on the internet their real name, or where they lived, or how old they were. Their father and I know that the internet is full of unknowable people who seem plausible and safe, and are not. We know because we were both lied to and stalked as adults.

My son Baz has been playing a multiplayer online game called Roblox for over a year now. His peers there know him by a nickname derived from his email address. They don’t know or care that he is in elementary school. They care whether he can script a door lock or use chat at speed, which he can. Roblox uses a small monthly fee to comply with the federal regulations about children under 13 being online only with parental permission. Free services cannot verify that users are over 13, and so habitually lock them out. This is miserable for my young digital native. He would like to be able to move as freely through the internet as anyone else, not exist in child-oriented ghettos.

To be able to move freely and with some degree of safety, he needs to be pseudonymous. He needs to lie about his birthdate. He needs to be able to shield his identity while he is easily victimized, and change it in the future to separate his preteen self from his professional self.

I want him to be able to make stupid mistakes without them being findable by his future employers, college professors, or dates. The internet is forever, but as long as we don’t use our employee names, we will not get turned down for work because of the online equivalent of facial tattoos.

My name is Heidi Waterhouse. I am teaching my children to be safe and pseudonymous. I think it matters.
