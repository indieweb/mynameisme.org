My name is Far McKon

My name is Far McKon. I’m a Linux driver developer, a community organizer, and a proud citizen of this amazing but often messed up country. I have co-founded hacker-spaces and a housing cooperative, and volunteered for civic causes from education through 4th of July parades.

The name Far started out as my mononym email address and standard handle after college, and soon I realized everyone knew me by that name. My business cards, my personal correspondences, and most of my accounts are all in my name, Far. In fact my Drivers License, Passport, and paychecks are about the only things that use the name I was born with.

For a variety of reasons I let the name ‘Far’ grown and overgrow my legal name like Morning Glory growing on an old chain link fence. I’ll just name the two most important here.

Names strongly prompt people to identify gender/ethnicity/home-country. Many times when I meet someone for the first time IRL (in real life) I hear from them “Oh, I thought you were a hippie|Indian|Foreigner!” and it makes me smile. I grew up as a middle class white male in 20th century USA, and as such I was born into the luckiest 1% of people on the planet. I think it is far better when first impressions are centered on what people do and say, rather that assumptions on where they were born or who they were born to.

By using a pseudonym I hope to disarm people’s most immediate assumptions on my own gender/ethnicity/home-country. I think it does a lot undermine some of those unearned and dangerous class assumptions, and emphasis my actions, not my luck in the great birth lottery.

I have to admit that when I type all of that out it sounds a bit political and kooky. Which is probably why I don’t often talk about that reason as often as I should. It is political and kooky, but my experience (and some good research) indicates it’s also true.

Secondly I use a pseudonym as a firewall. As part of the hacker community I must to admit I often do pretty crafty web-searches on even casual acquaintances. I know quite well how much information an automated script and an internet connection can dig up on people. It’s pretty frightening. By using a pseudonym I make at least a small firewall between my sociable and professional life vs my political, personal, financial life.

The single thing that bothers me most about this are the asinine claims that pseudonyms are un-American. That is uninformed bullshit. It is a small-minded and idiotic assumption which highlights our collective failure as a culture to think before we open our big mouths.

People using pseudonyms helped lay the groundwork for this country. Much of the constitutional debates were conducted by pamphleteers writing under pseudonyms. The Federalist Papers by ‘Publius’ helped convince people to form this amazing country. The Enlightenment ideals about democracy on which the USA was founded owe much to ‘Voltaire’. ‘Mark Twain’ was the sarcastic conscience of earlier America, the John Stewart of his day. Can you spot the pseudonyms?

Pseudonyms are American, and and have been used by Americans of all politics and backgrounds. Consider these great American pseudonyms: Ayn Rand, Dr. Seuss, Lady Gaga, The Big Bopper, Jon Bon Jovi, and ‘Superman’. Or how about Tupac Shakur, Lemony Snicket, Mother Jones and Josephine Baker? All of them 100% American, and 100% pseudonyms, and that is just the tip of the iceberg.
