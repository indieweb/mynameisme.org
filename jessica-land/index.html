My name is Jessica Land

My name is Jessica Land. I am a sex workers’ rights activist and writer of deeply personal memoir and occasional pornography. I worked at the historic Foundation for Research on STDs in New York City for five years, providing HIV prevention and social services to street-based sex workers. I served as Board Chair of the Sex Workers Outreach Project East, and worked with the Board of Directors for the Sex Workers Outreach Project and The Desiree Alliance. I have written about issues concerning the sex industry for $pread Magazine and www.BoundNotGagged.com.

A pseudonym assisted me in all of the activities listed above. I continue to use multiple pseudonyms online for various aspects of my work, though I am most known for sex workersâ€™ rights activism as Jessica Land. I first ventured online at the age of fourteen, and the use of a pseudonym was suggested by my grandfather, as a way to keep me safe while under the age of eighteen. Over time, my chosen name evolved, and my preference for the use of a name that might shield my legal identity grew.

Currently, Jessica Land is the name I use in daily life; offline friends call me by that name and have for over a decade. I am fortunate to have family and friends who support my work as a sex workers’ rights activist, and I don’t have to hide my online name from them. While I am very lucky, many of my colleagues are not in the same situation. They must protect themselves from family, employers, disgruntled clients, and stalkers. Further, in order to be honest in sharing their lived experience as sex workers, they must be careful to avoid criminal repercussions as a result of the information they share online.

Sex workers face enormous stigma in mainstream society. The ability to educate others by sharing their experience is a critical step towards abolishing stigma against sex work and ending criminalization of the consensual adult sex trade. Even in societies where sex work is fully decriminalized, safety concerns still mandate the use of pseudonyms for many. Violence against sex workers is rampant world-wide, and folks are wise to protect their home and loved ones by using an alternate identity online, for business, activism, or both.

Even though I am open as a sex workers’ rights activist, I keep two other identities, both related to writing, under wraps. I do not share that information with anyone, including my closest friends, because I want the freedom to express myself without worrying about what certain people might think, whether the topic is salacious porn writing or depressing memoir. Without the use of pseudonyms, I feel confident that my life would be less fulfilling and I would be much further from accomplishing my goals. When the various facets of my identity feel threatened, I internalize the threat and feel as though my mental, emotional, and physical safety are under attack. In order for people such as myself to freely participate in online communities, and provide needed, valuable discourse, we must feel safe, and pseudonyms are a vital part of that safety.
