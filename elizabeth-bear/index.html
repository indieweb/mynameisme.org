My name is Sarah Wishnevsky

My name is Sarah Wishnevsky, and I am a multiple-award-winning science fiction and fantasy writer. I have over a dozen novels in print. I have won two Hugos and a Sturgeon award and I’m currently nominated for a World Fantasy Award.

You’ve never heard of me because most of the online and publishing world knows me as Elizabeth Bear.

I chose to use a pseudonym for practical reasons: “Wishnevsky” is challenging for most people to spell and pronounce. “Bear”–which is one of my middle names: the other is in fact “Elizabeth”–is easy, and it–or “eBear”–is the name by which most people in fandom and in my professional life know me. I’ve been “Elizabeth Bear” online for over a decade, and “matociquala” for close to two decades. If you’re looking for a traceable identity, a longstanding online reputation (dating back to USENET and Delphi!), then you’re going to find it–for me–as Elizabeth Bear.

The internet is full of people who have much better reasons to use an online or professional pseudonym than I do–personal safety, integrity, to protect their careers. Online reputations are not built on a show of government-issued I.D. They are built on consistency of behavior over years, even decades–built on participation in relationships, in networks, in communities.

Online reputations are as real as anything you can learn about someone from a credit report.

I am who I say I am. And I support the right of others to be who they say they are, too.
