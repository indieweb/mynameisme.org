My name is Roger Baker

My name is Roger Baker. I am a pastor in the United Methodist Church. An important element of our faith is social activism. As a religious leader, I am expected to push boundaries and explore new ways we can express our faith. I am required to set the example by speaking up and speaking out. My faith pushes me towards vocal activism on issues of disability (I am disabled), human suffering in the face of warfare, and personal religious liberty.

My faith can be used against me when threat are made against my church, church members, or members of my broader faith community. Anonymity online helps blunt this threat. It allows me to speak frankly to challenge ideas with which I disagree by creating an environment of free exchange minus physical confrontation. The ideas confront each other while the individuals remain physically separate, allowing thoughts and words to challenge one another instead of fists and threats of force. This is a priceless boon of the Internet age. How can I challenge ignorance when my family, my livelihood, and my position in the church could be threatened if I say something that offends a congregant or church hierarch?

Imagine how the unhampered and uncensored exchange of ideas frees us. It allows us to shed light on the darkest recesses of human existence without undue risk. Of course, to right some of these wrongs, risk must be taken, but taking action rightly involves risk. Sharing ideas should not involve the risk of force.
