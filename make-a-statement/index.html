Make a statement

We’re looking for people who can speak persuasively and with authority on the subject of online pseudonymity and the freedom to choose your own name.

Our goal is to change the policy of companies who have persistently shown that they don’t understand the diversity of online culture, nor the needs of ordinary people. While thousands and thousands of people worldwide feel strongly about this issue and have spoken about it in a variety of forums, they’ve largely been ignored. Therefore, we’re especially looking for statements from people who are famous or prominent in their fields, or those who can speak on behalf of populations who need to choose their own names. This is not to say that we don’t value comments from everyone, just that we are focusing on those statements which we believe will be most effective in changing policy.

So, we’d particularly like to hear from:

  • Well-known performers (musicians, actors, etc), especially those who use stage names
  • Published authors, especially those who use pen names
  • Religious leaders (of any religion)
  • Political leaders or prominent political activists
  • Academics in relevant fields (law, sociology, media studies, etc)
  • Established professionals in relevant fields (health professionals, educators, etc)
  • People who run large “social” websites or online services

And representatives of groups such as:

  • Professional associations
  • Civil rights groups
  • Domestic violence support groups
  • Groups working with children at risk
  • Disability advocacy groups
  • Immigrant rights groups
  • … and any other organisation working with people who commonly want the freedom to choose their own name online

We’re also interested in hearing from anyone who represents a group or category we have not yet covered (you can see the list of categories in our sidebar), or from parts of the world that are not yet well represented.

If you fit the above descriptions, please fill out the form below.
