My name is Kage Alan

My name is Kage Alan. I’m a son, a partner, a caregiver to my father who is suffering from Alzheimer’s, and an author of several comedic gay novels.

I’ve written under a pseudonym for 16 ½ years now and for a multitude of reasons. It was, first and foremost, so that nobody would connect my partner to me or anything remotely gay since his employers then and now offer no protections for LGBT employees. I also did it and continue to do it for our privacy. It’s far too easy for someone to find private details about you on the web and in the hands of the wrong sort of people, far too easy for them to find you, find your employer, harass you, harass the people where you work and make things wholeheartedly unpleasant.

I keep my private life and my public life separate. Social media offers a wonderful way to find readers, meet other authors and keep in touch with everyone. It also offers temptation to internet stalkers and individuals with a less than upstanding agenda. A pseudonym is hardly the epitome of protection, but it does offer some and it allows me to maintain some distance between my persona as an author and that of my private self.

To remove the choice of using another name, regardless if someone is an author or part of the LGBT community, is to make that person vulnerable. We need to be able to look at the world around us, comment on it, share experiences, ask questions and explore in a way that encourages us to take chances and grow, not leave us worrying if our family, extended relatives, friends, acquaintances and even employers will come down on us for them. We lead our own lives, not theirs.
