My name is Latoya Peterson

My name is Latoya Peterson. I edit the blog Racialicious.com, and make my living as a writer.

I, like most of the folks over at Racialicious, blog under my government name. This wasn’t exactly the most conscious decision – when I decided to blog under my real name, I suppose I didn’t think any one would be paying attention. I had used pseudonyms before, mostly for journalling and online commenting. Those pseudonyms I used I consider extensions of my identity. I reserve the right to adopt a pen name if I so choose.

The reason why I support self-naming is because of the heavy consequences that come with talking about unpopular subjects. Many of our blogger friends adopted other names to be able to write freely about racism without reaping consequences in their personal or professional lives. Some of our commenters and contributors are celebrities, who want to illuminate something, but do not want to tie those comments to their professional name – especially if they are whistleblowing. Other people choose to open up online about instances of rape, sexual assault, physical trauma, their personal and sexual relationships, racist abuses at their schools or workplaces – when they do this, they are sharing their stories. They did not sign up to be martyrs for a cause, or to have these events tied to their identities forever.

I understand the idea of creating a better online space is complicated. People have done horrendous things under the guise of anonymity. But forcing everyone to only use “real names” (or at least, what looks like a real name – I could easily flip my name to something like Jane Jacobs, and that would be a way to camouflage my identity in a way that is approved by Google+, Facebook, and similar sites) and booting off people with no other cause is not the way to go about this.
